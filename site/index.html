Racing at 7,000 feet

Respect the Altitude

Santa Fe sits higher than Denver. The thin air is part of the challenge — and the charm. A little planning goes a long way.

Arrive early if you can

Coming from low elevation? Two to three days in town before the race helps your body adjust — and Santa Fe is a fine place to spend them.

Hydrate all week

The high desert is dry and you lose more fluid than you notice. Keep a water bottle in hand for a few days before the gun.

Pace the first four miles

The climb to the 7,330 ft high point comes early. Run it a touch slower than goal pace — the net-downhill back half is where you make it up.

Frequently Asked Questions

The short answers, all in one place. Everything here is covered in more detail up the page.

When and where does the race start?

Sunday, September 20, 2026, at 7:30 AM Mountain time, from La Tienda Plaza in Eldorado (7 Caliente Rd). It's a point-to-point course via Old Las Vegas Highway, finishing at Railyard Park (740 Cerrillos Rd) in the heart of Santa Fe.

How long is the course, really?

13.109 miles, USATF-certified, and net-downhill: you start at 6,992 ft, top out at 7,330 ft near mile 4, and finish at 6,956 ft. See the full elevation profile.

Is there a time limit?

Yes — 3 hours 30 minutes.

Where do I pick up my packet?

At the expo at Old Warehouse 21, next to Railyard Park: Friday 12–6 PM and Saturday 10 AM–5 PM. Race-morning pickup runs 5:45–7:10 AM at the Eldorado start.

How do I register?

On RunSignup — registration is open now.

How do I find my time after I finish?

Chip times post live as you cross the line. Open the live leaderboard and type your bib number into "Find my time" — past editions are on RunSignup results.

Will there be race photos?

Yes, free ones — from the start line in Eldorado to the Railyard finish. Links to all the galleries are in the Photos section.

I live at sea level. How bad is the altitude?

You'll feel it — Santa Fe sits higher than Denver. Arrive a couple of days early if you can, hydrate all week, and pace the first four miles gently. Full advice in Respect the Altitude.
