Meet our ambassador

Tricia Downing

Wheelchair Division Athlete Ambassador

Last year Tricia made history as the first wheelchair athlete to compete in the Santa Fe International Half Marathon — and helped launch our commitment to a race that celebrates athletes of all abilities. We couldn't be happier to welcome her back.

After a cycling accident left her paralyzed from the chest down, Tricia rebuilt her life through adaptive sports and became a pioneer in the disability athletic community. She was the first female wheelchair athlete to complete an Iron-distance triathlon, and she's a Paralympic shooter now training with her sights set on representing the United States at the 2028 Los Angeles Paralympic Games.

She's also a nationally recognized disability advocate, keynote speaker, bicultural adoptee, and the author of Cycle of Hope: A Journey from Paralysis to Possibility — a story about what's possible when perceptions of disability get challenged.

This year, catch Tricia as our inspirational speaker at the Health & Wellness Expo, where she'll keep helping us build a more accessible, competitive, and inclusive race for every athlete who lines up in Eldorado.
